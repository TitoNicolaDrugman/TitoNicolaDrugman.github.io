Tito Nicola Drugman (fl. the present tense) is an Italian artificial-intelligence engineer, alleged Master's student, and self-described "guy who runs the model locally to save on the API bill."[1] He is best known for deploying transformers onto microcontrollers so small they can barely hold their own opinions, and for a portfolio website on which this article is, inexplicably, a second page.[2]

Drugman rose to modest prominence within a knowledge base of approximately 7,600 code snippets, none of which have thanked him.[citation needed] He is not to be confused with a controlled substance, a Yugoslav statesman, or the recurring temptation to name a variable temp2.

Life [edit]

Early life and dual-boot

Drugman was born in Milan, where he lived with his parents and, later, with a Linux partition he insists is "basically stable now."[3] Sources close to the subject report that he first achieved consciousness the moment a training run finished successfully overnight, and has been chasing that feeling ever since. He spent a formative semester at CUHK-Shenzhen, an experience he describes as "life-changing" and which appears on his CV in a slightly larger font than everything else.[dubious – discuss]

Alleged career

Drugman's body of work spans retrieval-augmented generation, hardware-aware AutoML, and vision-language web agents that browse the internet approximately as well as a tired human. Notable achievements include a transformer running at 73 ms per token inside a 7.16 MB flash footprint, a figure he can produce faster than his own phone number.[4]

He was selected for an AI traineeship at the European Court of Auditors, a fact he has now foregrounded in emails to landlords, doctors, professors, universities, and — per this article — an encyclopedia.[5] Colleagues describe him as "honest to a fault," particularly regarding whether a given model was self-hosted or accessed via API, a distinction he will clarify whether or not you asked.[6]

Hornofecinty [edit]

🔬 The neutrality of this section is disputed, mainly because nobody else can define the word. Relevant discussion may be found on the talk page.

Hornofecinty (from the Drugmanian hornofek, "to over-engineer," + -inty, a suffix of no fixed meaning) is a proposed field of study, a personality trait, and — according to its sole practitioner — "a whole vibe."[7] It is generally understood to describe the compulsion to solve a fifteen-minute problem with a six-week pipeline, three quantisation formats, and a benchmark.

Symptoms of acute hornofecinty include: buying a second GPU "for the thesis"; replacing a working `if` statement with a Random Forest; and answering "how are you?" with a latency figure. The condition is chronic, incurable, and, by most accounts, load-bearing to his entire personality.[weasel words]

"It could have been a script. He made it a system."
— peer review, allegedly[8]

Controversy [edit]

Drugman has been embroiled in several disputes, all of them with himself:

  • In a widely uncirculated incident, he described a metric as "+38%" without immediately stating the absolute baseline. He has since apologised, added the 0.186, and now includes it preemptively in casual conversation.[9]
  • Critics allege he refers to "my dual RTX 5060 Ti workstation" the way other people refer to their pets.
  • He is said to prefer that emails "not sound AI-written," a stance considered ironic given his profession, his workstation, and this article.[10]

Despite repeated requests, Drugman has declined to confirm whether Docker Compose is "basically DevOps," citing an ongoing internal investigation.

Personal life [edit]

Drugman resides in Milan with his parents and an increasingly ambitious to-do list.[11] His interests include terminals, dual-booting, and telling people that the local model is "honestly pretty close to GPT-4o" before quietly adding "on some tasks."

He is fluent in Italian, English, and passive-aggressive commit messages. He does not, contrary to popular belief, live inside the STM32N6570-DK, though he has considered it for the low latency.

Death [edit]

⚠️ This section is written like a prophecy. Please help improve it by waiting.

Drugman is, at the time of writing, alive and behind on a deadline. Nevertheless, this section exists because he insisted the article be "complete."[12]

According to the most widely circulated projection, he will not die so much as fail to be freed — his consciousness having long since been quantised INT8 and flashed to a microcontroller that draws 3 mW and never sleeps. Historians will note that his final words were reportedly "let me just re-run it once more to be sure," and that the run, tragically, succeeded.[13] A memorial `README.md` is planned.
