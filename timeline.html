P.01

On-Prem RAG for LLM-Based Code Generation

Sep 2025 – Feb 2026
github.com/TitoNicolaDrugman/RAG-Code-Generation ↗
  • Designed a reproducible ingestion, chunking and indexing pipeline over a 7,600+ document knowledge base — corpus-agnostic and transferable to legal/audit domains.
  • Benchmarked three single-hop retrievers (BM25, dense CodeBERT embeddings, hybrid via Reciprocal Rank Fusion) plus two LLM-driven multi-hop pipelines (query decomposition, iterative refinement).
  • Deployed fully on-premises on a dual RTX 5060 Ti workstation, zero external API calls — 4-bit NF4 quantisation (bitsandbytes) to fit CodeLlama-7B-Instruct in available VRAM.
  • Result: +38% relative CodeBLEU (0.1859 → 0.2567) over the no-retrieval baseline; multi-hop decomposition reached 0.2505.

P.03

Transformer on the NPU of an STM32N6

Mar – Sep 2025
github.com/TitoNicolaDrugman/Transformer-NPU-STM32N6 ↗
  • Designed, trained and INT8-quantised a custom 4.56M-parameter encoder-only transformer from scratch for autoregressive text generation.
  • Applied per-channel symmetric INT8 quantisation of weights and activations against a held-out calibration set — the same discipline that governs compressing larger LLMs for on-prem inference.
  • Full pipeline: PyTorch model design, training on ~1.4M words from Project Gutenberg, C-code generation via STM32CubeIDE / X-Cube-AI, NPU configuration and on-device validation.
  • On-board results: 73.14 ms/token, 13.67 tokens/s, 257/407 ops NPU-accelerated, inside a 7.16MB flash footprint. Built with Patrizio Acquadro.

P.04

GenHack 2025 — De-biasing ERA5 Urban Heat

Oct – Nov 2025
github.com/TitoNicolaDrugman/GENHACK ↗
  • Three-person team hackathon (École Polytechnique × BNP Paribas × Kayrros): corrected systematic Urban Heat Island bias in ERA5-Land climate reanalysis data.
  • Fused four heterogeneous sources — ERA5-Land, Sentinel-2 NDVI, ECA&D ground stations, JRC Global Surface Water — into a unified 3M+ row dataset across IT/ES/FR/PT.
  • Compared Random Forest vs. HistGradientBoosting with a station-based split to prevent geographic leakage; the dominant predictors turned out to be elevation and latitude/longitude, not urban fraction.
  • Result: RMSE 3.01°C → 1.94°C (−35.5%), MAE −45%, systematic urban bias corrected by −93% on hold-out stations.
